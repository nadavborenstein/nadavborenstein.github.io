About me

I am a post-doc researcher in Computer Science at the University of Copenhagen, where I work on multimodal fact-checking and misinformation detection.

I am a post-doc researcher at the University of Copenhagen supervised by Professor Isabelle Augenstein. Before joining the CopeNLU group I’ve completed an MSc and a BSs in Computer Science and Computational Biology at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. I have also held the position of a Deep Learning tech lead at Lightricks.

I am passionate about creating methods to help both practitioners who use Neural Networks and the researchers who develop them to gain insights into why and how those models arrive at certain predictions. This, I believe, will increase the credibility and trust of ML tools in the public's view and promote the development of more capable and less biased models.

In my Ph.D., I work on the explainability and interpretability of NLP models and developing NLP methods for studying historial documents. I also take part in the Sapere Aude's EXPANSE project, for learning to explain attitudes voiced on social media.

Recent Publications

Here you can find my most recent publications and pre-prints.

Can Community Notes Replace Professional Fact-Checkers?

We studied the relationship and interdependence between professional fact-checkers and Community Notes contributors in fact-checking social media posts.
Proceedings of ACL 2025

Investigating Human Values in Online Communities

We trained a language model to extract moral values from over 20,000 subreddits. Our analysis reveals new insights into the relationship between human values and online communities.
Proceedings of NAACL 2025

LLM Tropes: Revealing Fine-Grained Values and Opinions in Large Language Models

We develop a novel methodology to extract political opinions and values from LLMs using tropes: semantically similar, recurrent phrases in the responses.
Findings of EMNLP 2024

What Languages are Easy to Language-Model? A Perspective from Learning Probabilistic Regular Languages

We investigate the empirical ability of RNN and Transformer LMs to learn of regular, probabilistic languages with various complexity parameters.
Proceedings of ACL 2024

Can Transformers Learn n-gram Language Models?

We study transformers' ability to learn random n-gram LMs to test whether theoretical lower bounds imply learnability of formal languages.
Proceedings of EMNLP 2024

Factcheck-Bench: Fine-Grained Evaluation Benchmark for Automatic Fact-checkers

This work presents Factcheck-Bench, a holistic end-to-end framework for annotating and evaluating the factuality of LLM-generated responses
Findings of EMNLP 2024

PHD: Pixel-Based Language Modeling of Historical Documents

In this paper we explore an exciting alternative to the processing of historical documents with NLP tools – utilising OCR-free, pixel-based language models.
Proceedings of EMNLP 2023

Multilingual Event Extraction from Historical Newspaper Adverts

We introduce a novel dataset of events extracted from multilingual historical newspapers, and use it to benchmark several LMs and training configurations.
Proceedings of ACL 2023

Measuring Intersectional Bias in Historical Newspapers

This study investigates intersectional biases in historical Caribbean newspapers, tracing their evolution and dynamics over a 250-year period.
Findings of ACL 2023

Imitation of Life: A Search Engine for Biologically Inspired Design

This paper presents a novel search engine based on a large-scale biological database to assist designers in finding biologically inspired solutions.
Proceedings of AAAI 2024

How Did This Get Funded?! Automatically Identifying Quirky Scientific Achievements

In this whimsical study, we curate and analyze a dataset of amusing scientific papers, developing NLP models to automatically identify them using hand-crafted features.
Proceedings of ACL 2021

Temporally stable video segmentation without video annotations

I co-authored this paper while working at Lightricks, where I implemented the crowd-annotation platform and analysed the resulting data.
